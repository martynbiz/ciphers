Ciphers

Ciphers have a long history, dating back to ancient civilizations, where methods like the Caesar cipher were used to encode messages and protect sensitive information

Over time, cryptography evolved, with more complex algorithms such as the Vigenère cipher and Enigma machine being developed during World War II

Today, ciphers are still crucial in modern security systems, playing a key role in encryption protocols that protect digital communication, online banking, and sensitive data storage, with widely used algorithms like AES (Advanced Encryption Standard) continuing to safeguard privacy in the digital age.

As technology advances, the future of cryptographic algorithms is likely to include quantum-resistant methods, as quantum computing threatens to break current encryption systems, driving the development of new, more secure algorithms that can withstand the capabilities of future computing power.
